When I first entered the world of mechanical keyboards, I was fascinated by the endless possibilities for customization. The satisfying *thock* of the switches, the heavy feel of an aluminum case, the precise geometry of the keycaps—it was all beautifully engineered. 

But as I spent more time looking at my desk, I realized something was missing. 

The mechanical keyboard community is largely dominated by industrial designs: sharp edges, aggressive sci-fi themes, skulls, dragons, and heavy metallic aesthetics. While I appreciate the craftsmanship behind these designs, they didn't resonate with the atmosphere I wanted to create in my own workspace. 

I didn't want my desk to feel like a command center or a spaceship. I wanted it to feel like a cozy cafe in Kyoto on a rainy afternoon. I wanted something soft, playful, and warm. I wanted a companion.

That desire is exactly why [Oai Studio](https://oai.studio) was born.

## The Search for Softness

I started looking for artisan keycaps that matched my aesthetic—something kawaii, gentle, and story-driven. I found a few, but I quickly realized that achieving true "softness" in a rigid plastic keycap is incredibly difficult. 

Standard manufacturing processes favor sharp lines and flat surfaces. To create a keycap that looks like a fluffy marshmallow, a freshly baked piece of toast, or a chubby little bear, you have to break away from traditional keyboard geometry. 

I decided that if I couldn't find exactly what I was looking for, I would have to make it myself.

## From Clay to Keyboard: Our Handmade Process

The journey wasn't easy. I set up a small studio in Kyoto, surrounded by the city's rich history of traditional craftsmanship. I wanted to bring that same level of care and attention to detail to the modern mechanical keyboard.

Our process at Oai Studio is deeply personal. It doesn't start on a computer screen; it starts with physical clay and pencil sketches. I spend hours shaping tiny figures, trying to capture a specific feeling—the comfort of a [Picnic Basket](https://oai.studio/products/picnic-fruit-basket-the-cream-bear-oai-studio), or the refreshing chill of [Grape Soda](https://oai.studio/products/grape-soda-the-little-cream-bear-oai-studio).

Once the physical feeling is right, we translate the design into 3D software to ensure it perfectly fits a Cherry MX switch. We use advanced full-color 3D resin printing to capture the intricate details, but the machine only does half the work. 

The true "softness" comes from the hand-finishing process. Every single [artisan keycap](https://oai.studio/collections/artisan-keycaps) that leaves our studio is inspected, detailed, and coated by hand. We apply a specialized transparent finish that gives our characters a warm, glossy, almost candy-like appearance. 

This handmade approach means that no mass production is involved. When you adopt a keycap from Oai Studio, you are receiving a piece of functional art that was crafted with human hands.

## Introducing the Cream Bear

The culmination of this philosophy is our signature creation: the [Cream Bear](https://oai.studio/collections/frontpage). 

I designed the Cream Bear to be the ultimate desk companion. With its rounded edges, chubby cheeks, and sweet pastel colors, it is the exact opposite of an aggressive, industrial keycap. It sits quietly on your Esc key, offering a tiny, comforting presence during long workdays or intense gaming sessions.

Whether it's the [Pudding Boat](https://oai.studio/products/pudding-boat-the-cream-bear-oai-studio) variant or the cozy [Toast](https://oai.studio/products/oai-studio-toast-cream-bear-keycap) edition, each bear carries a little piece of our Kyoto studio's heart.

## A Space for Creativity

Your desk is where you create, work, and dream. The tools you use should reflect your personality and bring you joy. 

I started Oai Studio because I wanted softer keycaps. But what I really wanted—and what I hope to share with you—is a workspace that feels like a sanctuary. A place where a tiny, handmade companion is always there to make you smile.
