Here is the complete guide on how to clean, maintain, and protect your [Oai Studio](https://oai.studio) companions so they stay beautiful for years to come.

## 1. How to Safely Mount and Remove Artisan Keycaps

The most common way artisan keycaps get damaged is during installation or removal. Resin is incredibly detailed and beautiful, but it can be more brittle than standard keyboard plastics.

*   **Never pull by the sculpt:** When removing an artisan keycap, never pinch the top of the figure (like pulling the ears of a [Cream Bear](https://oai.studio/collections/frontpage)). This can snap delicate parts.
*   **Always use a keycap puller:** Use a wire keycap puller (not the cheap plastic ring pullers, which can scratch the sides). Slide the wires under the base of the keycap and pull straight up with gentle, even pressure.
*   **Watch the stem fit:** Oai Studio keycaps are designed for Cherry MX stems. If the fit feels too tight, *do not force it*. Forcing a tight keycap can crack the resin stem. (Pro tip: If a resin stem is slightly too tight, you can soften it by holding it near a hairdryer for 5-10 seconds before mounting).

## 2. Routine Dusting (Weekly)

Dust is the enemy of detailed artisan keycaps. It settles into the tiny crevices of a [Little Panda's ramen bowl](https://oai.studio/collections/little-panda-kitchen) or the textured surface of a [Toast Cream Bear](https://oai.studio/products/oai-studio-toast-cream-bear-keycap).

*   **The Makeup Brush Method:** The absolute best tool for routine dusting is a clean, soft, fluffy makeup brush (like an eyeshadow or blush brush). Gently sweep it over the keycap once a week. The soft bristles will get into the details without scratching the glossy finish.
*   **Avoid compressed air on delicate parts:** While compressed air is great for blowing crumbs out of your keyboard case, a strong blast from a can of air held too close can sometimes damage very thin resin details. If you use air, keep the nozzle at least 6 inches away.

## 3. Deep Cleaning (Every 3-6 Months)

If your keycap has accumulated oils from your fingers or stubborn grime, it’s time for a deep clean.

**What you need:**
*   A bowl of lukewarm water
*   A drop of mild dish soap (do not use harsh detergents or alcohol)
*   A soft microfiber cloth
*   A soft-bristled toothbrush or makeup brush

**The Process:**
1.  Remove the keycap from the keyboard.
2.  Mix a single drop of dish soap into the lukewarm water. **Never use hot water**, as high heat can warp or soften resin.
3.  Dip the soft brush into the soapy water and very gently scrub the keycap. 
4.  Rinse the keycap under a gentle stream of cool water.
5.  Pat dry with the microfiber cloth and let it air dry completely before putting it back on your keyboard.

## 4. Protecting the Glossy Finish

All [Oai Studio keycaps](https://oai.studio/collections/artisan-keycaps) are sealed with a specialized transparent coating that gives them their signature candy-like gloss and protects the hand-painted details.

*   **Keep away from direct sunlight:** Prolonged exposure to intense UV rays (direct sunlight from a window) can cause resin to yellow over time and degrade the glossy finish. Keep your keyboard in a shaded area of your desk.
*   **No Isopropyl Alcohol:** Never use rubbing alcohol, hand sanitizer, or harsh chemical wipes to clean your artisan keycaps. Alcohol can eat through the protective clear coat and make the resin cloudy or sticky.

## Treat Them Like Tiny Art Pieces

With just a few minutes of care each month, your artisan keycaps will remain the shining centerpiece of your desk setup. Treat them not just as keyboard parts, but as the tiny, handmade pieces of art that they are.
